What is SERP Overlap?

SERP Overlap measures how many domains rank for both keywords. High overlap (>60%) means Google considers the keywords very similar — they might cause keyword cannibalization. Low overlap means the keywords target different intents and should have separate pages.

Why does this use 2 queries?+
Each keyword requires a separate Google SERP lookup. The tool fetches both, then compares the domain lists locally. With the free tier (5/day), you can do 2 overlap comparisons.
What is keyword cannibalization?+
Keyword cannibalization occurs when multiple pages on your site compete for the same keyword. High SERP overlap between two keywords suggests they target the same intent, so having separate pages may split your ranking potential.
How does the overlap score work?+
The overlap score is the percentage of domains that appear in the top results for both keywords. A score above 60% indicates strong similarity in search intent, while below 30% suggests distinct intents.
When should I merge content?+
Consider merging pages when overlap exceeds 60% and neither page ranks well individually. Consolidating into one comprehensive page can concentrate ranking signals and improve performance for both keywords.
Can I compare more than two keywords?+
The tool compares two keywords at a time. For broader analysis, run multiple comparisons to map out clusters of related keywords and identify which ones share search intent.
