FAQ

What is a SERP preview tool?+
It simulates how your page appears in Google search results — title, URL, description — helping you optimize for click-through rate before publishing.
Why does pixel width matter more than character count?+
Google truncates titles and descriptions based on pixel width, not character count. A title with wide characters like "W" or "M" gets cut sooner than one with narrow characters like "i" or "l". This tool measures actual rendered pixels for accuracy.
What is the ideal title tag length?+
Keep titles under 580 pixels on desktop (~60 characters) and 500 pixels on mobile. Titles that exceed this limit get truncated with an ellipsis (...), which can hurt click-through rates.
What is the ideal meta description length?+
Meta descriptions should stay under 920 pixels on desktop (~155 characters) and 780 pixels on mobile. Descriptions that are too short may look incomplete, while too-long ones get cut off.
Does Google always use my title and description?+
Not always. Google may rewrite your title or generate its own description if it thinks its version better matches the search query. Well-optimized, relevant meta tags are much more likely to be used as-is.
How does the AI suggestion feature work?+
The AI analyzes your page content and auto-detects target keywords with real search volumes. It then generates optimized title and description options that fit within Google's pixel limits while incorporating your primary and secondary keywords.
Can I use this tool for any website?+
Yes. Enter any URL and click "Fetch from URL" to pull the current title and description. The tool works with any publicly accessible webpage — no signup or installation needed.
